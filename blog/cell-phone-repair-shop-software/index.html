What repair shop software actually does

Before comparing anything, it helps to be clear about what this category of software is built to do. Every platform in this article — RepairDesk, Orderry, RepairShopr, CellSmart — is designed to manage the work you already have. Repair tickets, parts inventory, customer communication, invoicing, payments, technician assignment.

None of them are designed to get you new customers. That is not a criticism. It is just the reality of what the category does. If your shop is busy and you are losing track of repairs, missing parts, or struggling to keep customers updated, this software solves that problem well. If your shop is quiet and you need more customers walking in, repair shop software will not help with that. You need a different tool for that problem — which we will get to at the end.

The gap none of them fill

Look at that comparison table again. Every platform has repair ticketing, inventory, SMS updates. They all do the same core things reasonably well. But scroll down to the bottom rows and you see the same answer for all four: no website, no SEO pages, no device quote calculator, no online lead capture.

This is not an oversight. It is just not what these tools were built to do. They were built to manage the customers already inside your shop. That is a legitimate and valuable thing to do. But it means if your shop is quiet, none of these tools solve your actual problem.

The problem most repair shops have is not that their repair process is disorganized. It is that when someone in their city searches "iPhone repair near me" or "sell iPhone for cash near me" at 10pm, the shop does not show up. That customer goes somewhere else. The repair shop software companies know this. They just do not build that product.

The real question to ask before buying any repair software: Is your shop busy and you need to manage it better? Buy RepairDesk or Orderry. Or is your shop quiet and you need more customers? Fix the website and the Google presence first. Software that manages your operations will not bring in a single new customer.

What you need alongside any repair shop software

If you pick one of the tools above and get your operations running smoothly, the next thing that moves your revenue is getting more people through the door. That requires three things none of the software above provides.

A website with pages that rank on Google. Not one generic page — specific pages for every repair you offer and every device you buy. Each page targets a real search your customers are making. iPhone repair in your city. Sell iPhone near me. Battery replacement cost. Without those pages, you are invisible to the people searching for you right now.

A device quote calculator. If you buy phones, the single highest-return thing you can add to your website is an instant quote calculator. A customer searching to sell their iPhone gets a number immediately, locks the price with their email, and shows up the next morning. You capture the lead before they drive to a kiosk or go online. No repair shop software does this for you.

Google Business Profile that is actually optimized. Most shops set up their Google Business Profile once and forget it. Right categories, complete services list, consistent reviews, active posts — these are what get you into the map pack that shows at the top of every local repair search. Repair shop software does not touch any of this either.

The bottom line

For managing repairs you already have, RepairDesk is the most capable and widely used. Orderry is the easiest to get started with. RepairShopr is the right choice if you do a lot of IT work alongside repairs. CellSmart makes the most sense if retail is a meaningful part of your operation.

But before you spend $80 to $150 a month on any of them, ask yourself honestly whether your problem is that your operations are disorganized or whether your problem is that not enough customers are finding you. If it is the second one, no repair shop software in this article is going to fix it. You need a website that works, a Google presence that ranks, and a lead capture system that pulls in customers when the shop is closed.

Most shops need both eventually. Just make sure you are solving the right problem first.

Common questions

What is the best software for a cell phone repair shop?
For managing repairs you already have, RepairDesk is the most widely used and has the most integrations with parts suppliers. For shops that want something easier to learn, Orderry is cleaner and faster to set up. For shops that do both repair and retail, CellSmart is built specifically for that combination. All of them manage operations but none help you get new customers — that requires a separate website and lead capture system.
How much does cell phone repair shop software cost?
Most repair shop software runs between $69 and $149 per month per location. RepairDesk starts at $79 per month. Orderry starts at $69 per month. RepairShopr starts at $69.99 per month. CellSmart pricing requires a demo call. Most offer a free trial so you can test before committing.
Does repair shop software help you get more customers?
No. Repair shop software is designed to manage customers who are already inside your shop. It handles tickets, inventory, invoices, and communication. Getting new customers requires a website that ranks on Google, a device quote calculator that captures leads online, and SEO pages targeting local repair and buyback searches. Those are separate tools that repair shop software does not provide.
What is RepairDesk used for?
RepairDesk is a point-of-sale and repair management system for cell phone and computer repair shops. It handles repair ticketing, parts inventory, customer communication, invoicing, and payments. It integrates directly with parts suppliers like MobileSentrix and Injured Gadgets so you can order parts from within the platform. It is one of the most used platforms in the industry globally.
